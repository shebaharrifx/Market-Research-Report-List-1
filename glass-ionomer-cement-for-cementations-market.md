<p><p>Glass Ionomer Cement (GIC) for cementations is categorized into three main types: Powder, Liquid, and Set. </p><p>Powder refers to the material in dry form, which is mixed with a liquid to initiate the binding process. The Liquid component contains an aqueous solution of polyacrylic acid, which activates the powder's setting characteristics. Once mixed, it transitions into the Set form, a hardened, stable material used for various dental applications, including restorations and cementation of crowns and bridges, known for its adhesion and fluoride release.</p></p>
